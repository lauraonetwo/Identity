# Identity
How does a society get millions of strangers to cooperate with each other?

## Main questions
### What properties should a perfect identity have?

- Unique.
- Difficult to forge.
- Easy to prove.
- Easy to revoke if compromised.
- Private.
- Portable.
- Permanent.
- Not dependent on a single organization.

Does anything with all these properties exist?

No.

We've already found a flaw.

### Who controls identity?

Today it depends on the context.

- For travel: the State.
- For work: the company.
- For study: the university.
- For payments: the bank.
- For internet access: Google or Apple, often.

There is no single owner.

Your identity is fragmented.

### Is identity a fact or a relationship?

Imagine you live alone on an island.
Do you need an identity?

No.

Identity emerges when another person exists.

It's a social relationship.

Not an object.

That connects computer science with sociology.

### Does identity change?

Yes.

When you were born:

- you had no education
- you couldn't drive
- you couldn't vote

Today you do.

So... Are those abilities part of your identity?

It seems so.

Identity isn't fixed. It's dynamic.

### What part of our identity should be public?

Very little.

Think about it.

When you buy alcohol, all you need to know is:

✔ You're of legal drinking age.

You don't need to know:

- Your name
- Your address
- Your ID number
- Your photograph

Today we reveal far more than necessary.

### Can identity exist without trust?

No.

All identity depends on trust.

We trust that:

- the passport is authentic
- the bank verifies customers
- the university doesn't issue fake degrees

Then another huge question arises.

Is identity really about people...
...or about trust?
